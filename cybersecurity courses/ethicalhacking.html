Course Overview

The Ethical Hacking Basics course provides an introduction to the field of ethical hacking and its significance in cybersecurity. In this course, you will learn the fundamental concepts of networking as required for ethical hacking. You will explore the techniques used to gather information about target systems through footprinting and reconnaissance. The course covers network scanning and enumeration methods to identify vulnerabilities. You will also learn about system hacking and how to defend against Trojans, viruses, and worms. The course delves into sniffing and social engineering attacks. Additionally, you will gain insights into web application security and how to protect web applications from cyber threats. By the end of this course, you will have a solid understanding of ethical hacking basics and be prepared to pursue advanced certifications in the cybersecurity domain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this course suitable for beginners with no prior cybersecurity knowledge?

Yes, this course is designed for beginners and assumes no prior cybersecurity knowledge.

Will I be able to perform ethical hacking after completing this course?

This course provides the foundational knowledge required to begin a career in ethical hacking. Further specialization and hands-on experience are recommended to become a professional ethical hacker.

What tools will be used in this course?

The course will cover various open-source and commercial tools commonly used in ethical hacking, such as Nmap, Wireshark, and Metasploit.

Are there any prerequisites for this course?

No prerequisites are required for this course. Basic computer knowledge is sufficient.
